\# Crossover Stockton — Track Semantics



The Crossover system routes users into one of four tracks based on their immediate situation and needs.



\## Track A (Red) — Emergency / Prevent Housing Loss



For individuals or families at immediate risk of losing housing or basic stability.



Must support:

\- Employment continuity

\- Pets

\- Vehicles and RV living

\- Rapid diversion and stabilization



\## Track B (Yellow) — Transitional / Working / Vehicular



For individuals who are employed or employable, living in vehicles, or stabilizing after crisis.



Focus:

\- Short-term housing options

\- Employment-compatible resources

\- Storage, hygiene, and mail services

\- Vehicle-safe living



\## Track C (Green) — Life Maintenance / Autonomy / With Nature



For individuals choosing outdoor living or maintaining autonomy outside institutional shelter systems.



Includes:

\- Nature-oriented or low-barrier resources

\- Digital ID support

\- Mail, phone, and documentation services

\- Personal agent or advocate access



\## Track D (Blue) — Medical / Clinical Care



For individuals with urgent medical, psychiatric, or substance-related needs.



Includes:

\- Detox and stabilization

\- Mental health crisis care

\- Hospital and urgent care routing
